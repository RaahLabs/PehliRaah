10 short interactive experiences. About 25 minutes. Not a test — a clearer picture of how you think, decide, and respond in real situations.

What this is

Raah uses simple decision‑based games to observe patterns in how people approach risk, planning, fairness, and collaboration. There are no right answers and no labels.

Game 1 of 10

🎈 Saahas

saahas — courage, nerve

In this experience, you’ll repeatedly decide whether to push a situation a little further or stop and secure what you have.

Some people naturally push ahead. Others prefer to pause early. There is no correct way to play — just go with what feels natural.

Game 2 of 10

🤝 Saajhedaari

saajhedaari — partnership

Here, you’ll be paired with another player. You won’t communicate — both of you choose at the same time.

Sometimes cooperation helps. Sometimes it doesn’t. Just make the choice that feels right in each moment.

Game 3 of 10

🗼 Yojna

yojna — planning

This task has a clear goal, but the path isn’t immediately obvious. Every move changes what’s possible next.

Some people plan first. Others learn as they go. Both approaches work — notice how you respond.

Game 4 of 10

👀 Nazariya

nazariya — perspective

You’ll read short everyday situations involving people at work and choose what feels closest to what’s really happening.

There are no trick answers. Trust your first read — not analysis.

Game 5 of 10

⚡ Thaam

thaam — restraint

Things will appear quickly. Sometimes you should act. Other times, waiting is the better move.

This isn’t about speed. It’s about noticing when to move — and when to pause.

Raah reflects behavior observed during short, interactive experiences. Results describe tendencies in this session only and should be interpreted alongside other information.
